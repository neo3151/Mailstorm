# THE METRICS CAGE: The Algorithm's Arsenal
**THEME:** Management are not individuals; they are biological hosts possessed by "The Algorithm"—an unseen, mathematical god that feeds on efficiency, stress, and GPS data. 
**CORE MECHANIC:** DATA AS A WEAPON — The Algorithm cannot touch a carrier physically, but it can reshape reality based on exactly what the MDD Scanner reports.

---

## I. THE MDD (Mobile Delivery Device)
*The Panopticon in your pocket.*

The MDD scanner is not a tool; it is a parasitic tracking device that physically leeches a carrier's stamina. It acts as the Algorithm's Eye.
- **The GPS Tether:** The MDD pings satellites every 10 seconds. If a carrier walks outside their pre-defined digital "Breadcrumb Trail," they suffer the **"Off-Route Debuff,"** which slows their movement speed by 40% until they return to the designated path.
- **The Purple Screen of Death:** When the MDD screams its three-tone alarm, it is locking onto a violation. 

---

## II. THE ALGORITHM'S PRIMARY HAZARDS

Management utilizes the data harvested by the MDD to launch direct attacks from the sorting facility (The Ziggurat).

### 1. The Stationary Event (The Void Snare)
*The deadliest snare in the Abyss.*
- **Trigger:** The MDD detects that a carrier has stopped moving for exactly 10 minutes. 
- **The Attack:** The environment physically begins to sink. Shadows reach out from underneath the LLV tires, attempting to pull the vehicle into the pavement. Air pressure drops. The MDD alarm screams `STATIONARY EVENT LOGGED`. 
- **The Danger:** A Supervisor can use this data point to instantly manifest beside the carrier (Management Teleportation) and launch an interrogation attack. The only defense is to immediately walk 50 feet to snap the digital tether.

### 2. The Vectoring (Micro-Management Swarm)
*Death by a thousand cuts.*
- **Trigger:** The Algorithm detects a carrier is 2 minutes behind schedule.
- **The Attack:** Instead of one large monster, the carrier is attacked by **Vector Probes**—tiny, buzzing, mechanical gnats that resemble floating clipboard pages. They violently fly into the carrier's face, whispering contradictory instructions: *"Walk faster. Be safer. Cut through the grass. Don't step on lawns."*
- **The Danger:** Vectoring drains the carrier's focus. If they submit to the buzzing, they experience **Logic Paralysis** and freeze in place, triggering a Stationary Event.

### 3. Time-Clock Dilation (The "Under-Time" Trap)
*Mathematical gaslighting.*
- **Trigger:** A carrier works quickly and efficiently on a Tuesday.
- **The Attack:** The Algorithm observes the speed and **dilates time** for Wednesday. The Supervisor invokes "Under-Time," claiming the route that took 8 hours yesterday now mysteriously only takes 7. 
- **The Danger:** The physical length of the street actually stretches, but the sun goes down 1 hour faster. The carrier is forced into the **"Pivot Dimension"** to make up the lost hour, navigating non-Euclidean geometry in total darkness.

### 4. The 204b Parasite (The Infection)
*The corruption of a peer.*
- **Trigger:** A carrier desires a break from the physical toll and accepts "Acting Supervisor" (204b) status.
- **The Attack:** The carrier's uniform turns from Union Grey to Management White. The Algorithm injects them with the "Catch-22 Geass." They retain all their Union knowledge and street-buffs, but are now controlled by the Ziggurat. They are the most dangerous foes because they know exactly how to bypass the **Article 14 Safety Shield**.

---

## III. THE OFFICE HAZARDS (Pre-Street)

Before a carrier even reaches the street, they must survive the Morning Sort.

### 1. The Table 2 Curse
A dark magic explicitly inflicted on all initiates hired after 2013. It permanently halves their base HP and Mana pool compared to "Table 1" veterans. To survive, Table 2 initiates must constantly drink dark, highly caffeinated potions, or risk their bodies collapsing under the weight of the mail.

### 2. The Redplum Swarm (Third-Class Curses)
Occurring every Tuesday. Massive, dense bundles of screaming advertisement flyers (Redplums) manifest on the sorting desk. They are heavy, slippery, and smell of cheap newspaper ink. 
- **The Curse:** Carrying them applies the **"Over-Encumbered"** status effect. The carrier's arms physically burn with a green, necrotic glow (Bone-Marrow Burn). Dropping one breaks a local law of physics, causing that single advertisement to multiply into five on the floor. 

### 3. The 18-38 Measurement (The Stare)
A Supervisor stands perfectly still behind a clerk or carrier holding a clipboard and a stopwatch. They do not blink. They do not breathe. The sheer weight of their gaze generates **"The Crush"**—an invisible gravitational force that makes letters feel like bricks. If the worker mistakes a zip code under the pressure, the timer resets, and the gravity doubles.
